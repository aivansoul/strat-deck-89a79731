# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Belgian independent professionals and small practices — doctors, lawyers/notaries, garage owners, hair & beauty salons, real-estate agents, restaurant owners. They are busy, phone-dependent, and lose revenue on every missed call. They evaluate SpitUP from a desktop or phone between appointments, in French or Dutch (also EN/DE), and their job-to-be-done is: "make sure every call is answered professionally without hiring a secretary."

## Product Purpose

SpitUP is Belgium's hybrid AI + human telesecretariat: a conversational AI answers 24/7 in FR·NL·EN·DE, and human secretaries in Brussels take over live on sensitive calls. The website's job is to (1) rank and be cited for télésecrétariat queries in Belgium — classic SEO and generative-engine (GEO) answers alike — and (2) convert visitors into quote requests. Site architecture: one pillar page (telesecretariat-ia.html) + six profession spoke pages, each with a voicebot demo, interlinked as a topic cluster.

## Brand Personality

Premium, warm, proudly Belgian. Confident but never cold — "IA + humain, en duo" is the signature; the human side always shows (real team photos and footage, named testimonials). Editorial rather than corporate; precise rather than flashy.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: purple gradients, identical icon-card grids, stock illustrations, template energy.
- Cheap call-center sites: cluttered, discount-y, generic headset stock photos, aggressive popups — the low-cost télésecrétariat competitors.

## Design Principles

1. **The duo is the story** — every page shows AI *and* human together; never present SpitUP as a bot alone.
2. **Prove it, don't claim it** — real client footage, named testimonials (Axelle Bronckart, SD Worx), measured numbers with dates; no unfounded ratings.
3. **Belgian by design** — bilingual-ready copy, local cities, RGPD/INAMI compliance surfaced as first-class content, prices in EUR.
4. **Answer-first writing** — each section opens with a self-contained, citable answer (SEO/GEO); structure is content, not decoration.
5. **Premium restraint** — one gold accent on warm neutrals, editorial type, few but deliberate motions; luxury is precision, not effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA: ≥4.5:1 body contrast, keyboard-navigable interactive elements with visible focus, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation (marquee, counters, video sequences), semantic landmarks, `lang` attributes per language version.
